Here's a timeline of Hunter S. Thompson's life

  • 1937 - Born in Louisville, Kentucky, to a middle class family.
  • 1952 - His father passed away; he was 14.
  • 1956 - Joined the United States Air Force.
  • 1958 - Honorably discharged from the Air Force. This rather "early" discharge was due to his rebellious nature and tendency to disobey orders with one superior quoted as saying, "Sometimes his rebel and superior attitude seems to rub off on other airmen staff members."
  • 1960 Wrote his first novel, The Rum Diary which was not published until 1998.
  • 1963 - Tie the knot with Sandra Cooklin
  • 1964 - His son, Juan Fitzgerald Thompson was born.
  • 1967 - Publish his book, Hell's Angels: The Strange and Terrible Saga of the Outlaw Motorcycle Gang ; became internationally well-known for this.
  • 1970 - Ran unsuccessfully for sheriff of Pitkin County, Colorado. It was around this time his style of writing, "Gonzo journalism" was born.
  • 1971 - Publish what is arguably his best work, Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas which was later adapted into a film of the same name in 1998, starring Johnny Depp.
  • 1973 - Published Fear and Loathing on the Campaign Trail of '72.
  • 1980 - Divorced Sandra Cooklin; they remained good friends nevertheless. This year also marked the release of the movie, "Where the Buffalo Roam" which loosely depicts his rise to fame. He became good friends with one of the actors on set, Bill Murray.
  • 1998 - A film adaptation of his novel, "Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas" was released. He also became close friends with Johnny Depp who played Raoul Duke in the film.
  • 2003 - Tie the knot with Anita Bejmuk.
  • 2005 - Died from a self-inflicted gun wound.

Interesting facts about Hunter S. Thompson

  1. He was cremated and had his ashes shot out of a cannon as per his final wish. The funeral costed $3 million and was funded by his good friend, Johnny Depp.
  2. He left a frozen elk's heart on his friend's, Jack Nicholson, doorstep as a gift for his birthday. He also blasted a recording of a pig being eaten by bears while he fired gunshots into the air outside the actor's home.
  3. Invented the sport of shotgun golf. Here's a video of the sport.
  4. He got into a fight with a Hells' Angels member. This fight occurred when Hunter saw a Hells' Angels' member, "Junkie" George, physically assaulting his wife and dog. Upon seeing this, he stated,"Only a punk beats his wife and dog!"
  5. He was a gun fanatic. There were even tales of him "signing" books by firing at them.
